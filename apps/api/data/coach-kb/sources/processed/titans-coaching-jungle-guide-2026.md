## Cleaned notes by concept

### 1. Time is the core currency of jungle

The video keeps returning to the idea that jungle is fundamentally about managing windows of time. The jungler should not think of the role as "ganking" first. The role is about using time to get on the map faster than the opponent, convert actions into pressure, and chain that pressure into towers, objectives, vision, or camps.

Reusable idea:

- jungle decisions should be judged by whether they create tempo, deny tempo, or waste time

### 2. Tracking is broader than locating the enemy jungler

Tracking is presented as more than "where is the enemy jungler?" It includes:

- draft expectations
- summoner spells
- matchup dynamics
- which lanes will have priority
- what the next likely enemy objective or gank is

Useful reusable framing:

- strong tracking combines map information with pre-game prediction

### 3. Conversion matters more than the kill itself

The video defines a "conversion ratio" as the relationship between:

- actions
- kills or forced summoners
- permanent gains such as plates, objectives, vision, camps, or tower pressure

This is useful for Don Sosa Coach because it aligns with a strong principle:

- kills are temporary
- permanent advantages win games

### 4. Mental stability is staying present

The useful part of the mental section is not the motivational framing. The useful part is:

- instability is not only tilt
- instability also means autopilot
- unstable players choose based on emotion instead of reason

Good reusable translation:

- mentally stable jungle play means staying present enough to choose rationally between paths, fights, and trades

### 5. Use a mother rule for empty actions

One of the strongest pieces in the transcript is a simple decision filter:

Every action should answer:

1. Do I gain time?
2. Do I deny enemy resources?
3. Do I convert this into something permanent?

If the answer is no to all three, the action is probably empty.

This is highly reusable because it gives the player a stable review heuristic.

### 6. Improve only a few KPIs at once

The video argues that players should pick only a small number of KPIs per week rather than trying to improve every weakness at once.

The reusable insight is:

- consistency improves faster when the player tracks a small set of controllable habits

### 7. Avoidable deaths early are catastrophic

The transcript strongly emphasizes that avoidable deaths in the early game are one of the cleanest reasons players fail to climb. The exact numbers in the video should be treated carefully, but the conceptual takeaway is strong:

- dying early destroys tempo
- dying early often removes objective control
- reducing free deaths is one of the fastest ways to stabilize jungle performance

### 8. Recalls before objectives are part of the objective itself

The video repeatedly emphasizes that objective fights are often won before the fight starts. The most reusable pieces are:

- reset early enough to arrive with gold spent
- establish vision before the spawn
- position before the fight begins

Good translation for the KB:

- objective setup begins 30 to 90 seconds before spawn, not when the monster is already being hit

### 9. Keep the champion pool narrow enough to repeat your system

A useful coaching idea here is not the exact champion recommendations, but the principle that a pool should be small enough to preserve consistency in:

- clear speed expectations
- route logic
- tempo patterns
- first item spikes

For Don Sosa Coach this becomes:

- simplify the pool until your early route and gameplan stop changing every match

### 10. Simple tracking loop

The transcript offers a practical simplified tracking loop:

- where did the enemy start?
- what camps are available to them?
- which lanes have priority?
- what is their next realistic objective or play?

That is useful because it is teachable and repeatable.
