## Cleaned notes by concept

### 1. Break distance versus range before the poke becomes permanent

One of the clearest lane ideas in the source is that into ranged or pseudo-ranged top matchups, the melee player cannot allow repeated free damage before ever getting to interact. The winning idea is:

- break distance early enough
- force the lane into an all-in or close-range trade before losing too much HP

### 2. Use advantage to deny both CS and XP

The transcript repeatedly highlights a high-level punishment pattern:

- once ahead, do not only take last hits
- stand where the enemy must walk into your damage to take minions
- if possible, deny not only gold but also experience from key minions

This is extremely reusable for many lanes, not just Camille.

### 3. A winning lane still has to respect jungle state

A major teaching point is that hard winning the 1v1 does not remove the rest of the map. When the enemy jungler is known to be on your side, your lane advantage does not justify random overextension. You still need to read:

- jungle side
- reset timings
- whether your own jungler can cover

### 4. Movement pathing can trap the enemy's exit

Some of the strongest trade descriptions are about where the player's clicks are aimed. The point is not only to hit abilities, but to move into a position where the enemy's escape or disengage tools still leave them in a losing corridor.

### 5. Map information can justify actions that look greedy

There are moments in the source where staying on the wave at low HP looks troll in isolation but is actually correct because the map proves there is no punish available. That turns into a broader rule:

- low HP decisions must be judged with map context, not visual fear alone

### 6. Side pressure should create impossible choices

One of the strongest macro ideas in the source is that a fed side laner should put the enemy team into no-win situations:

- defend the side and lose pressure elsewhere
- ignore the side and lose tower, wave, or a vulnerable carry

This is not only top lane knowledge. It is general side pressure theory.

### 7. If you are the carry, your death can end the game

The transcript is very clear that once a player becomes the central source of pressure, dying once can erase the game state. This is a very strong carry principle:

- once your lead is the map's stabilizer, staying alive is part of your macro job

### 8. Wait for your real engage window

There is a reusable all-in principle in the clip where the player refuses to keep walking forward without the key cooldown available. The lesson is broad:

- if your champion's engage tool or key spell is down, do not roleplay the aggressive posture as if you still had it

### 9. Use enemy vision expectations against them

The transcript shows a useful deception pattern:

- show one path while the enemy has vision
- leave vision
- then take a different path using sweeper or fog

That creates picks because the enemy keeps playing from the old assumption.
